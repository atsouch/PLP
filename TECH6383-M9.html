Personal Learning Plan (PLP)

Introduction

As a doctoral student pursuing an Ed.D in instructional technology with over three decades of experience developing platforms, training teachers, and consulting on e-learning projects across 60+ countries, I have identified key growth areas to advance my career. Despite my expertise in K-12 Computing curriculum development, emerging technologies require further mastery to achieve my aspirations.

My five-year goal is to attain a senior leadership position as a Chief Learning Officer or Director of Educational Innovation. Within ten years, I aim to establish myself as a global authority in instructional technology implementation through roles in international organizations, university leadership, or as founder of an educational technology research institute. Advanced expertise in emerging technologies will support these goals through evidence-based frameworks, scalable professional development models, and demonstrated impact on learning outcomes.

Research shows that personal learning networks foster meaningful, lasting connections and bridge formal and informal learning experiences (Atwell, 2007). As Oddone et al. (2019) demonstrate, developing a structured approach to personal learning is essential for professional advancement in educational technology.

Conclusion: Implementation Plan and Motivation Strategies

Implementation begins April 2025, allocating 8-10 hours weekly to PLP activities divided between coursework, self-directed learning, implementation projects, and documentation. The sequence starts with AI-based adaptive learning (April-September 2025), followed by data analytics (July 2025-February 2026) and PLE design (October 2025-March 2026).

This strategic plan addresses critical needs in my career trajectory by filling knowledge gaps in emerging technologies, providing formal credentials, creating evidence of implementation success, and building a professional network of experts. Kennedy (2018) supports that connectedness in personal learning networks takes shape as motivation, learning, and identity.
